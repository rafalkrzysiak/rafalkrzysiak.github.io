About Rafal Krzysiak

I am currently a Mechanical engineering Ph.D. student at University of California - Merced. My research is in Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI) empowered sensors and smart sensing. The main application of my research is in, but not limited to health monitoring; such as explainable cardiovascular disease prediction/prevention. In addition to my academic studies, I collaborate with NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory as a mechanical engineer where I design, analyze, simulate and build instruments used for planetary science missions; on Earth and off. In my free-time, whenever I am able to create the time, I enjoy my hobby of astrophotography during the night time. While whenever I get the chance to, I enjoy breaking away from technology and going for a hike and enjoy nature.

About my work at NASA JPL

I have designed and machined components for a gas detection instrument to be used on the International Space Station to monitor CO2 levels. More recently, I am project lead for a water detection instrument to be used on the Lunar surface. Where the instrument is to be placed on mobile robots as well as in astronauts hands. The main objective is to detect the water concentration on the Lunar surface to map how much water is on the lunar surface and determine the distribution.
