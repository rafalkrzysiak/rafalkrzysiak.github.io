Conductive-Radiative Driven Digital Twin of Miniature Tunable Laser Spectrometer in Micro-Gravity

Abstract

Tunable laser spectrometers (TLS) have emerged as a pivotal tool in var- ious scientific and industrial applications, ranging from environmental moni- toring to medical diagnostics. Their precision and sensitivity, however, can be influenced by a myriad of factors, including environmental conditions such as temperature and component degradation. To address these challenges, the integration of a thermally conductive-radiative driven digital twin has become paramount. We developed a level-II digital twin to help better un- derstand the temperature performance of a TLS that will be deployed in the International Space Station micro-gravity environment. Our work comprised of an investigation utilizing thermal experiments with and without a thermal chamber to help develop a digital twin with accurate thermo-physical pa- rameters governing the temperature performance of the physical instrument.
