Advancing Multi-Task Learning with Fractional Order SGD and Quantus-Assessed Explainability for Planetary Health Monitoring

Abstract

This paper presents a novel framework for planetary health monitoring, integrating fractional order optimization and explainable AI (XAI) techniques. Using hyperspectral satellite imagery from the EuroSAT dataset, the approach enhances land use classification with multi-task learning (MTL) and index mapping for critical environmental metrics: Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI), Green NDVI (GNDVI), and Normalized Difference Water Index (NDWI). Central to the framework is Fractional Order Stochastic Gradient Descent (FOSGD), which achieves optimal performance at $\boldsymbol{\alpha=1.2}$, demonstrating 96\% classification accuracy, a 0.96 F1-score, and improved convergence compared to traditional optimizer methods. Explainability techniques like GradCAM, SmoothGrad, and Integrated Gradients provide interpretable insights into the model’s decision-making by identifying key features and regions. The Quantus framework evaluates the interpretability and reliability, with high scores in Faithfulness, Robustness, and Localization metrics. Preliminary results show improved robust index mapping and accurate classification, validating the model’s ability to focus on relevant geographical features and spectral bands. This study highlights the potential of interpretable AI for sustainable land management and planetary health.
