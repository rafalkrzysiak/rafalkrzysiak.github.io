A Proximal Point Sensing System for Mapping Soil Moisture Using A Miniaturized Spectrometer

Abstract

Surface soil moisture plays an important role in agricultural applications such as precipitation, irrigation, and evaporation. Several studies utilized hyper-spectral and multi-spectral sensors to capture images and then extract needed information from pixels. However, these procedures are expensive and have low accuracy since they require extracting information from images indirectly rather than directly. Therefore, in this paper, we propose a point sensing system by utilizing a miniaturized spectrometer, which enables proximal sensing benefits for sensing surface soil moisture. We validated our point sensing system by conducting a series of real-world experiments and using the ordinary Kriging method for generating surface soil moisture distributions. Results demonstrated using a spectrometer can acquire spectrum response, and the spectrum absorption magnitude has been revealed at the specific band directly.
